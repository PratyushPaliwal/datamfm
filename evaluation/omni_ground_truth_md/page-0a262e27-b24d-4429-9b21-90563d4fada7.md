Definition 4.1 (Divided Differences). The divided differences for a function $ f\left( x\right) $ are defined as follows:

$$
$$f[x_k] = f(x_k),$$
$$

$$
$$f[x_{k~~1}, x_k] = \frac{f[x_k]~f[x_{k~~1}]}{{x_k}~ x_{k~1}},$$
$$

$$
$$f[x_{k\quad2}, x_{k\quad1}, x_k] = \frac{f[x_{k\quad1},x_k]\quad f[x_{k\quad2}, x_{k\quad1}]}{x_k\quad x_{k\quad2}} ,\tag{13}$$
$$

$$
$$f[x_{k\quad 3}, x_{k\quad 2}, x_{k\quad 1}, x_k] = \frac{f[x_{k\quad 2}, x_{k\quad 1}, x_k]\quad f[x_{k\quad 3}, x_{k\quad 2}, x_{k\quad 1}]}{x_k\quad  x_{k\quad 3}} .$$
$$

The recursive rule for constructing higher-order divided differences is

$$
$$f\left\lbrack  {{x}_{k\;j},{x}_{k\;j + 1},\ldots ,{x}_{k}}\right\rbrack   = \frac{f\left\lbrack  {{x}_{k\;j + 1},\ldots ,{x}_{k}}\right\rbrack \quad f\left\lbrack  {{x}_{k\;j},\ldots ,{x}_{k\;1}}\right\rbrack  }{{x}_{k}\quad{x}_{k\;j}}\tag{14}$$
$$

and is used to construct the divided differences in Table 4.8.
