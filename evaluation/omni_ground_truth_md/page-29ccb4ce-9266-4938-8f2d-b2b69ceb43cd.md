## ROMAN SHVYDKOY

*Proof.* Let us first verify (121). For any $ {2k} + l \leqslant {2m},l \geqslant 1 $ , we apply (134) with $ K = k + \frac{l}{2} $ and $ L = l + 1 $ , and using that the neutral term is bounded,

$$
$${\mathrm{h}}_{q - {2k} - l}^{k,l} \lesssim  \mathop{\sum }\limits_{{i = 1}}^{N}{\left( {\int }_{\Omega  \times  {\mathbb{R}}^{n}}{\left| {D}_{x}^{k + \frac{l}{2}}f\right| }^{2}\langle v{\rangle }^{q - {2k} - l}\mathrm{\;d}v\mathrm{\;d}x\right) }^{{\theta }_{i}^{\prime }}{\left( {\int }_{\Omega  \times  {\mathbb{R}}^{n}}\mathop{\sum }\limits_{{{l}^{\prime } \leqslant  L}}{\left| {\partial }_{v}^{l + 1}f\right| }^{2}\langle v{\rangle }^{q - {2k} - l}\mathrm{\;d}v\mathrm{\;d}x\right) }^{{\theta }_{i}^{\prime \prime }},$$
$$

with $ {\theta }_{i}^{\prime } + {\theta }_{i}^{\prime \prime } < 1 $ . So, if $ l $ is even, then the $ x $ -integral on the right hand side consists of integer order derivatives and therefore,

$$
$${\mathrm{h}}_{q - {2k} - l}^{k,l} \lesssim  \mathop{\sum }\limits_{{i = 1}}^{N}{\mathrm{x}}^{{\theta }_{i}^{\prime }}{\mathrm{d}}^{{\theta }_{i}^{\prime \prime }}.\tag{123}$$
$$

If $\it { l }$ is odd, $l = 2 p + 1$, and hence $k + p + 1 \leqslant m$, we use interpolation in $x$ and distribute the weight accordingly,

$$
$${\int }_{\Omega  \times  {\mathbb{R}}^{n}}{\left| {D}_{x}^{k + p + \frac{1}{2}}f\right| }^{2}\langle v{\rangle }^{q - {2k} - l}\mathrm{\;d}v\mathrm{\;d}x \leqslant  {\int }_{{\mathbb{R}}^{n}}{\left( {\int }_{\Omega }{\left| {\partial }_{x}^{k + p + 1}f\right| }^{2}\mathrm{\;d}x\right) }^{1/2}{\left( {\int }_{\Omega }{\left| {\partial }_{x}^{k + p}f\right| }^{2}\mathrm{\;d}x\right) }^{1/2}\mathrm{\;d}v$$
$$

$$
$$=\int_{\mathbb{R}^{n}}\left(\int_{\Omega}|\partial_{x}^{k+p+1}f|^{2}\left\langle v\right\rangle^{q-2k-2p-2}\mathrm{d}x\right)^{1/2}\left(\int_{\Omega}|\partial_{x}^{k+p}f|^{2}\left\langle v\right\rangle^{q-2k-2p}\mathrm{d}x\right)^{1/2}\mathrm{d}v$$
$$

$$
$$\leqslant\left(\int_{\Omega\times\mathbb{R}^{n}}|\partial_{x}^{k+p+1}f|^{2}\left\langle v\right\rangle^{q-2k-2p-2}\mathrm{d}v\mathrm{d}x\right)^{1/2}\left(\int_{\Omega\times\mathbb{R}^{n}}|\partial_{x}^{k+p}f|^{2}\left\langle v\right\rangle^{q-2k-2p}\mathrm{d}v\mathrm{d}x\right)^{1/2}$$
$$

So, if $ k = p = 0 $ , then the above is bounded by $ \lesssim {\mathrm{x}}^{1/2} $, and if $ k + p > 0 $ , then by $ \lesssim \mathrm{x} $ . In either case we arrive at the same bound (123).

Summing up over $ {2k} + l \leqslant {2m},l \geqslant 1 $ , and reindexing the exponents, we obtain

$$
$$\mathrm{v} \lesssim  \mathop{\sum }\limits_{{i = 1}}^{N}{\mathrm{x}}^{{\theta }_{i}^{\prime }}{\mathrm{d}}^{{\theta }_{i}^{\prime \prime }},$$
$$

with $ N $ depending only on the indexes.

As to the mixed term, we have

$$
$${\int }_{\Omega  \times  {\mathbb{R}}^{n}}\langle v{\rangle }^{q - {2k}}{\partial }_{x}^{\mathbf{k}}f \cdot  {\partial }_{x}^{\mathbf{k} - 1}{\partial }_{v}f\mathrm{\;d}v\mathrm{\;d}x \leqslant  {\left( {\int }_{\Omega  \times  {\mathbb{R}}^{n}}{\left| {\partial }_{x}^{k}f\right| }^{2}\langle v{\rangle }^{q - {2k}}\mathrm{\;d}v\mathrm{\;d}x\right) }^{1/2}{\left( {\int }_{\Omega  \times  {\mathbb{R}}^{n}}{\left| {\partial }_{x}^{k - 1}{\partial }_{v}f\right| }^{2}\langle v{\rangle }^{q - {2k}}\mathrm{\;d}v\mathrm{\;d}x\right) }^{1/2}$$
$$

using (134) with $ K = k,L = {2k} $ ,

$$
$$\leqslant  {\mathrm{h}}^{1/2}\mathop{\sum }\limits_{i}{\left( {\mathrm{h}}_{q - {2k}}^{k,0}\right) }^{{\theta }_{i}^{\prime }/2}{\left( {\mathrm{\;h}}_{q - {2k}}^{0,{2k}}\right) }^{{\theta }_{i}^{\prime \prime }/2} \leqslant  \mathop{\sum }\limits_{i}{\mathrm{h}}^{{\theta }_{i}},$$
$$

where all $ {\theta }_{i} < 1 $ . After adding up the terms and relabeling the exponents, we obtain

$$
$$\mathrm{m} \lesssim  \mathop{\sum }\limits_{i}{\mathrm{h}}^{{\theta }_{i}}.$$
$$

It remains to collct the obtained inequalities and prove the generation of bound lemma.
