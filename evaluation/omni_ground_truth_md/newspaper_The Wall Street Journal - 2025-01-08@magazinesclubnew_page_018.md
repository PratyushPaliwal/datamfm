# GFL Environmental to Sell Services Business
In \$5.6 Billion Deal

# BY DENNY JACOB

GFL Environmental has
reached an agreement to sell its
environmental services business in a deal valued at 8 billion
Canadian dollars, equivalent to
about \$5.59 billion, though it
would retain an equity interest.

The Canadian company, which transports and recycles waste, among other services, said it reached the agreement with funds managed by affiliates of Apollo and BC Partners. It would retain a $44\%$ equity interest in the business, while funds managed by affiliates of Apollo and BC each would hold a $28\%$ equity interest.

GFL said its board, when excluding interested directors
who have recused themselves,
has unanimously approved the transaction. The deal is ex-

pected to close in the first quarter.

GFL expects cash proceeds
from the deal to be around
C\$6.2 billion. It intends to use
up to C\$3.75 billion of the net
proceeds to repay debt, leaving
up to C\$2.25 billion for share
repurchases. The balance will
go to transaction fees and general corporate purposes.

Chief Executive Patrick
Dovigi said a deleveraged balance sheet will provide GFL
with more financial flexibility to
deploy capital into organic
growth initiatives and solid
waste M&A and allow for a
greater return of capital to
shareholders.

Dovigi said the company will
maintain an option to repurchase the environmental services business within five years
of closing.
