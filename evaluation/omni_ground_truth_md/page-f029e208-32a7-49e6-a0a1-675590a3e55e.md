Now we define a Jacobi-Koszul-Vinberg manifold as a symmetric analogue of a Jacobi manifold.

DEFINITION 2.9. A *Jacobi-Koszul-Vinberg structure* on an affine manifold $ \left( {M,\nabla }\right) $ is a pair$(h,E)$in $ \Gamma \left( {{S}^{2}{TM}}\right) \times \mathfrak{X}\left( M\right) $satisfying, for any $ \alpha ,\beta ,\gamma $ in $ {\Omega }^{1}\left( M\right) $,

(i)$ \left( {{\nabla }_{{h}^{\sharp }\alpha }h}\right) \left( {\beta ,\gamma }\right) - \langle \alpha ,E\rangle h\left( {\beta ,\gamma }\right) = \left( {{\nabla }_{{h}^{\sharp }\beta }h}\right) \left( {\alpha ,\gamma }\right) - \langle \beta ,E\rangle h\left( {\alpha ,\gamma }\right) $,

(ii)$( \nabla _ { E } h ) ( \beta , \gamma ) - \langle \gamma , \nabla _ { h ^ { \sharp } \beta } E \rangle + \langle \beta , E \rangle \langle \gamma , E \rangle = 0$,

(iii)$ \nabla _ { E } E  = 0$.

$ \left( {M,\nabla ,h,E}\right) $ is called a*Jacobi-Koszul-Vinberg manifold.*
