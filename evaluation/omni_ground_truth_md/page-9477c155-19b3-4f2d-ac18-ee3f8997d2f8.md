# #### G.1.3. BOUNDS ON THE SECOND MOMENT ALONG THE FINITE-PARTICLE SYSTEMS

In this section, we show that along the finite-particle dynamics (16) and algorithm (20), the distributions remain in $ \mathcal{P}\left( {\mathbb{R}}^{2dN}\right) $ .

Under Assumption 1, recall from Theorem 35 (in Section H) that there exists a unique equilibrium point $ {z}^{ * } = \left( {{x}^{ * },{y}^{ * }}\right) \in {\mathbb{R}}^{2d} $ which satisfies $ \nabla V\left( {z}^{ * }\right) = 0 $ . Define $ {\mathbf{z}}^{ * } = \left( {{x}^{ * },\ldots ,{x}^{ * },{y}^{ * },\ldots ,{y}^{ * }}\right) \in  {\mathbb{R}}^{2dN} $ . Then by construction, $ {b}^{\mathbf{Z}}\left( {\mathbf{z}}^{ * }\right) = 0 $ , where $ {b}^{\mathbf{Z}} $ is the vector field defined in (17).

Lemma 31 Assume Assumption 1. Suppose $ {\mathbf{Z}}_{t} \sim {\rho }_{t}^{\mathbf{Z}} $ evolves following the finite-particle dynamics (16) in $ {\mathbb{R}}^{2dN} $ from $ {\mathbf{Z}}_{0} \sim {\rho }_{0}^{\mathbf{Z}} \in \mathcal{P}\left( {\mathbb{R}}^{2dN}\right) $. Then $ {\rho }_{t}^{\mathbf{Z}} \in \mathcal{P}\left( {\mathbb{R}}^{2dN}\right) $ for all $ t \geq 0 $ .

Proof We note that $ {\rho }_{t}^{\mathbf{Z}} $ is absolutely continuous with respect to the Lebesgue measure on $ {\mathbb{R}}^{2dN} $ by virtue of the Brownian motion component in the dynamics (16). We now show that the second moment of ${\rho }_{t}^{\mathbf{Z}}$remains finite for all $ t \geq 0 $ . Since $ {\mathbf{Z}}_{t} $ evolves following the dynamics (16), its density${\rho }_{t}^{\mathbf{Z}}$ evolves following the Fokker-Planck equation:

$$
$$\frac{\partial {\rho }_{t}^{\mathbf{Z}}}{\partial t} =  - \nabla  \cdot  \left( {{\rho }_{t}^{\mathbf{Z}}{b}^{\mathbf{Z}}}\right)  + {\tau \Delta }{\rho }_{t}^{\mathbf{Z}}.$$
$$

From this, we can compute, using integration by parts:

$$
$$\frac{d}{dt} \mathbb{E}_{\rho_t^\mathbf{Z}} \left[ \| \mathbf{Z}_t - \mathbf{z}^* \|^2 \right] = \int_{\mathbb{R}^{2dN}} \frac{\partial \rho_t^\mathbf{Z}(\mathbf{z})}{\partial t} \| \mathbf{z}- \mathbf{z}^* \|^2 d\mathbf{z}$$
$$

$$
$$= \int_{\mathbb{R}^{2dN}} \left( -\nabla \cdot \left( \rho_t^\mathbf{Z} b^\mathbf{Z})(\mathbf{z} \right) + \tau \Delta \rho_t^\mathbf{Z}(\mathbf{z}) \right) \| \mathbf{z} - \mathbf{z}^* \|^2 d\mathbf{z}$$
$$

$$
$$= {\int }_{{\mathbb{R}}^{2dN}}{\rho }_{t}^{\mathbf{Z}}\left( \mathbf{z}\right) \left\langle  {{b}^{\mathbf{Z}}\left( \mathbf{z}\right) ,\nabla \left( {\|\mathbf{z} - {\mathbf{z}}^{ * }\|}^{2}\right) }\right\rangle  d\mathbf{z} + \tau {\int }_{{\mathbb{R}}^{2dN}}{\rho }_{t}^{\mathbf{Z}}\left( \mathbf{z}\right) \Delta \left( {\|\mathbf{z} - {\mathbf{z}}^{ * }\|}^{2}\right) d\mathbf{z}$$
$$

$$
$$= 2 \int_{\mathbb{R}^{2dN}} \rho_t^\mathbf{Z}(\mathbf{z}) \left\langle b^\mathbf{Z}(\mathbf{z}), \mathbf{z} - \mathbf{z}^* \right\rangle d\mathbf{z} + \tau \int_{\mathbb{R}^{2dN}} \rho_t^\mathbf{Z}(\mathbf{z}) (4dN) d\mathbf{z}$$
$$

$$
$$= 2 \int_{\mathbb{R}^{2dN}} \rho_t^\mathbf{Z}(\mathbf{z}) \left\langle b^\mathbf{Z}(\mathbf{z}) - b^\mathbf{Z}(\mathbf{z}^*), \mathbf{z}- \mathbf{z}^* \right\rangle d\mathbf{z}+ 4 \tau d N$$
$$

$$
$$\leq -2\alpha \int_{\mathbb{R}^{2dN}} \rho_t^\mathbf{Z}(\mathbf{z}) \| \mathbf{z} - \mathbf{z}^* \|^2 d\mathbf{z} + 4\tau d N$$
$$

$$
$$= -2\alpha \mathbb{E}_{\rho_t^\mathbf{Z}} \left[ \| \mathbf{Z}_t - \mathbf{z}^* \|^2 \right] + 4\tau d N$$
$$

where the inequality above uses the property that $ - {b}^{\mathbf{Z}} $ is $ \alpha $-strongly monotone, from Lemma 12. We can write the differential inequality above equivalently as:

$$
$$\frac{d}{dt}\left( {{e}^{2\alpha t}{\mathbb{E}}_{{\rho }_{t}^{\mathbf{Z}}}\left\lbrack  {\|{\mathbf{Z}}_{t} - {\mathbf{z}}^{ * }\|}^{2}\right\rbrack  }\right)  \leq  {e}^{2\alpha t}{4\tau dN}.$$
$$

Integrating from 0 to $ t $ and rearranging the result gives:

$$
$$\mathbb{E}_{\rho_t^\mathbf{Z}} \left[ \| \mathbf{Z}_t - \mathbf{z}^* \|^2 \right] \leq e^{-2\alpha t} \mathbb{E}_{\rho_0^\mathbf{Z}} \left[ \| \mathbf{Z}_0 - \mathbf{z}^* \|^2 \right] + \frac{(1 - e^{-2\alpha t})}{\alpha} 2\tau d N$$
$$

$$
$$\leq \mathbb{E}_{\rho_0^\mathbf{Z}} \left[ \| \mathbf{Z}_0 - \mathbf{z}^* \|^2 \right] + \frac{2\tau d N}{\alpha}.$$
$$
