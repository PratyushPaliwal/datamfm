This was attributed to the presence of Ca-rich minerals in the shales occasioned by high levels of CaO and MgO. However, the slightly to moderately acidic condition formed by chemical weathering of shale coupled with soil forming processes have led to enhanced levels of these PTEs in the soil compared to concentrations in the shale bed-rock. Some fractions of the Cd and Pb are leached into aquatic environment to concentrations above the national standards. Fortunately,the enhanced levels in the soils are not mirrored in the crop plants.These were attributed to low solubility of Cd and Pb minerals that are formed in the surface environment. The low uptake could also be attributed to plant species, nature of the plants and media as selective uptake/accumulations of metals (Nganje et al., 2010).

Consumption of edible crop plants as well as ingestion of water

contaminated with Cd and Pb may be harmful to humans'health through time as a result of bioaccumulation.Prolonged exposure to low concentration of Cd and Pb could lead to kidney disease,lung damage, high blood pressure, fragile bones and nervous disorder (ASTDR 2018; Khan et al.,2013;Hu, 2002). Exposure of infants and children to concentrations of Pb above the maximum allowable level may delay physical and mental development and can cause slight deficits in attention span and learning abilities.
