## XINCHEN MIAO

5.1. Expansion in the $ \mathfrak{q} $-aspect. Since $ {\pi }_{1} $ and $ {\pi }_{2} $ are cuspidal automorphic representations, We note that $ {\Phi }_{1} = {\varphi }_{1}{\varphi }_{2}^{\mathfrak{q}} $ is a rapid-decay function which is invariant under the congruence group $ {\mathbf{K}}_{0}\left( {\mathfrak{u}\mathfrak{v}\mathfrak{q}}\right) $ , we apply Plancherel formula (See [Zac20, Theorem 2.8]) to the well-defined inner product (5.2) in the space of forms of level $ \mathfrak{mnq} $. We have the following decomposition of the considered period

$$
$${\mathcal{P}}_{\mathfrak{q}}\left( {\mathfrak{l},\Phi ,\Phi }\right)  = {\mathcal{G}}_{\mathfrak{q}}\left( {\mathfrak{l},\Phi ,\Phi }\right)  + {\mathcal{C}}_{1},\tag{5.3}$$
$$

where the generic part is given by

$$
$$\mathcal{G}_q(\mathfrak{l}, \Phi, \Phi) = \sum_{\substack{\pi ~\text{cuspidal} \\ c(\pi) | \mathfrak{uvq}}} \lambda_\pi(\mathfrak{l}) \sum_{\psi \in \mathcal{B}(\pi, \mathfrak{uvq})} \left| \langle \varphi_1 \varphi_2^\mathfrak{q}, \psi \rangle \right|^2$$
$$

$$
$$+ \mathop{\sum }\limits_{\substack{{\chi  \in  \widehat{F^{ \times  } \setminus  {\mathbb{A}}_{F}^1}} \\  {\mathrm{c}{\left( \chi \right) }^{2} \mid  \mathfrak{ uvq }} }}{\int }_{-\infty }^{\infty }{\lambda }_{\chi ,{it}}\left( \mathfrak{l}\right) \mathop{\sum }\limits_{{{\psi }_{it} \in  \mathcal{B}\left( {\chi ,{\chi }^{-1},{it},\mathfrak{ uvq }}\right) }}{\left| \left\langle  {\varphi }_{1}{\varphi }_{2}^{\mathfrak{q}},\mathrm{E}\left( {\psi }_{it}\right) \right\rangle  \right| }^{2}\frac{dt}{4\pi }.\tag{5.4}$$
$$

Moreover, the constant term $ {\mathcal{C}}_{1} $ is the one-dimensional contribution (constant term) which appears only if both $ {\pi }_{1} $ and $ {\pi }_{2} $ are cuspidal and there exists finite many quadratic characters $ \chi $ (depending on the number field $ F $) of $ {F}^{ \times } \setminus {\mathbb{A}}_{F}^{1} $ such that $ {\pi }_{1} \simeq {\pi }_{2} \otimes \chi \left( {\chi = 1}\right. $ if $ {\pi }_{1} = {\pi }_{2} $ for example). The cardinality of such quadratic character $ \chi $ is $ {O}_{\varepsilon ,F}\left( {\left( uvq\right) }^{\varepsilon }\right) = {O}_{\varepsilon ,F}\left( {q}^{\varepsilon }\right) $. For such a quadratic character $ \chi $ , by applying the Hecke relation (3.5),  we have the following:

$$
$$\mathcal C_1 = V_F^{-1} \cdot \langle T_\mathfrak l(\Phi), \varphi_\chi \rangle \langle \varphi_\chi, \Phi \rangle = \frac{\chi(\mathfrak l) \deg(T_\mathfrak l)}{V_F} \cdot \left| \langle \varphi_1 \varphi_2^\mathfrak{q}, \varphi_\chi \rangle \right|^2$$
$$

$$
$$= \frac{{\zeta }_{\mathfrak{q}}{\left( 2\right) }^{2}\chi \left( \mathfrak{l}\right) \deg \left( {\mathrm{T}}_{\mathrm{\mathfrak{l}}}\right) }{{\zeta }_{\mathfrak{q}}{\left( 1\right) }^{2}{V}_{F}} \cdot  {\left| \frac{1}{{q}^{1/2}}\left( \left\langle  {\varphi }_{1} \cdot  \left( {\mathrm{T}}_{\mathfrak{q}}{\varphi }_{2}\right) ,{\varphi }_{\chi }\right\rangle   - \frac{1}{{q}_{1}} \cdot  \left\langle  {\varphi }_{1} \cdot  \left( {\mathrm{T}}_{{\mathfrak{q}}_{1}^{m - 2}}{\varphi }_{2}\right) ,{\varphi }_{\chi }\right\rangle  \right) \right| }^{2}\tag{5.5}$$
$$

$$
$$= \frac{\zeta_\mathfrak{q}(2)^2 \chi(\mathfrak{l}) \deg(T_\mathfrak{l})}{\zeta_\mathfrak{q}(1)^2 V_F} \cdot \left| \frac{1}{q^{1/2}} \left( \lambda_{\pi_2}(\mathfrak{q}) - \frac{1}{q_1} \lambda_{\pi_2}(\mathfrak{q}_1^{m-2}) \right) \cdot \langle \varphi_1 \varphi_2, \varphi_\chi \rangle \right|^2$$
$$

$$
$$=\frac{\zeta_\mathfrak{q}(2)^2 \chi(\mathfrak{l}) \deg(T_\mathfrak{l})}{q \zeta_\mathfrak{q}(1)^2 V_F} \cdot \left| \left( \lambda_{\pi_2}(\mathfrak{q}) - \frac{1}{q_1} \lambda_{\pi_2}(q_1^{m-2}) \right) \right|^2 \cdot \left| \langle \varphi_1 \varphi_2, \varphi_\chi \rangle \right|^2,$$
$$

where $ {\mathrm{T}}_{\mathfrak{q}} $ is the Hecke operator and $ {\zeta }_{\mathfrak{q}}\left( s\right) \mathrel{\text{:=}} \mathop{\prod }_{{v \mid \mathfrak{q}}}{\zeta }_{{F}_{v}}\left( s\right) $ is the partial Dedekind zeta function. If the integral ideal $ \mathfrak{q} $ is squarefree, i.e. $ m = 1 $ , we define $ {\mathrm{T}}_{{\mathfrak{q}}_{1}^{m - 2}} \mathrel{\text{:=}} 0 $ and $ {\lambda }_{{\pi }_{2}}\left( {\mathfrak{q}}_{1}^{m - 2}\right) \mathrel{\text{:=}} 0 $ . Hence, $ {\mathrm{T}}_{{\mathfrak{q}}_{1}^{m - 2}}{\varphi }_{2} = 0 $ . Moreover, the degree of the Hecke operator $ {\mathrm{T}}_{\mathfrak{l}} $ is defined by

$$
$$\deg \left( {\mathrm{T}}_{\mathfrak{l}}\right)  \mathrel{\text{:=}} \frac{1}{{\ell }^{1/2}}\mathop{\sum }\limits_{{0 \leqslant  k \leqslant  \frac{n}{2}}}{\gamma }_{n - {2k}} = {\ell }^{1/2}\frac{\zeta _{F_{\mathfrak{p}}}(1)}{{\zeta }_{{F}_{\mathfrak{p}}}\left( {n + 1}\right) }  \leqslant  {\ell }^{1/2}{\zeta }_{{F}_{\mathfrak{p}}}(1) .\tag{5.6}$$
$$

Since $ {\pi }_{2} $ is $ {\theta }_{2} $-tempered at all the finite places, we have $ \left| {{\lambda }_{{\pi }_{2}}\left( \mathfrak{q}\right) }\right| \leqslant \left( {m + 1}\right) \cdot {q}^{{\theta }_{2}} $ . Applying Cauchy-Schwartz inequality,

$$
$${\mathcal{C}}_{1}{ \ll  }_{{\pi }_{1},{\pi }_{2},F,\varepsilon }{\left( \ell q\right) }^{\varepsilon }\frac{{\ell }^{1/2}}{{q}^{1 - 2{\theta }_{2}}}{ \ll  }_{{\pi }_{1},{\pi }_{2},F,\varepsilon }{\left( \ell q\right) }^{\varepsilon }\frac{{\ell }^{1/2}}{{q}^{{25}/{32}}}$$
$$

since $ 0 \leqslant {\theta }_{2} \leqslant \frac{7}{64} $ . Hence we can conclude

$$
$${\mathcal{P}}_{\mathfrak{q}}\left( {\mathfrak{l},\Phi ,\Phi }\right)  = {\mathcal{G}}_{\mathfrak{q}}\left( {\mathfrak{l},\Phi ,\Phi }\right)  + {O}_{{\pi }_{1},{\pi }_{2},F,\varepsilon }\left( {{\left( \ell q\right) }^{\varepsilon }\frac{{\ell }^{1/2}}{{q}^{1 - 2{\theta }_{2}}}}\right) ,\tag{5.7}$$
$$

where we recall that $ \Phi $ is defined as (5.1).
