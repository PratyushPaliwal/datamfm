Proof. For any $ {z}_{1},{z}_{2} \in \mathbb{C} $ , let $ {z}^{\theta } = \left( {1 - \theta }\right) {z}_{1} + \theta {z}_{2} $ and let $ \gamma \left( \theta \right) = f\left( {\left| {z}^{\theta }\right| }^{2}\right) {z}^{\theta } $ for $ 0 \leq \theta \leq 1 $ , we have

$$
$$f\left( {\left| {z}_{2}\right| }^{2}\right) {z}_{2} - f\left( {\left| {z}_{1}\right| }^{2}\right) {z}_{1} = \gamma \left( 1\right)  - \gamma \left( 0\right)  = {\int }_{0}^{1}{\gamma }^{\prime }\left( \theta \right) \mathrm{d}\theta , \tag{3.0.19}$$
$$

where

$$
$${\gamma }^{\prime }\left( \theta \right)  = \left( {1 + \sigma }\right) f\left( {\left| {z}^{\theta }\right| }^{2}\right) \left( {{z}_{2} - {z}_{1}}\right)  + g\left( {z}^{\theta }\right) \overline{\left( {z}_{2} - {z}_{1}\right) }, \tag{3.0.20}$$
$$

with

$$
$$g\left( z\right)  = \left\{  {\begin{array}{ll} {f}^{\prime }\left( {\left| z\right| }^{2}\right) {z}^{2} = {\beta \sigma }{\left| z\right| }^{{2\sigma } - 2}{z}^{2}, & z \neq  0, \\  0, & z = 0, \end{array}\;z \in  \mathbb{C}.}\right.  \tag{3.0.21}$$
$$

Then we have, since $ \sigma > 0 $ ,

$$
$$\left| {f\left( {\left| {z}_{2}\right| }^{2}\right) {z}_{2} - f\left( {\left| {z}_{1}\right| }^{2}\right) {z}_{1}}\right|  \leq  {\int }_{0}^{1}\left| {{\gamma }^{\prime }\left( \theta \right) }\right| \mathrm{d}\theta  \lesssim  {\left| {z}_{\theta }\right| }^{2\sigma }\left| {{z}_{1} - {z}_{2}}\right|  \leq  {M}_{0}^{2\sigma }\left| {{z}_{1} - {z}_{2}}\right| , \tag{3.0.22}$$
$$

which completes the proof.

We say $ F : \mathbb{C} \rightarrow \mathbb{C} $ is (globally) $ p $ -Hölder continuous with $ 0 < p \leq 1 $ if

$$
$$\left| {F\left( {z}_{1}\right)  - F\left( {z}_{2}\right) }\right|  \leq  C{\left| {z}_{1} - {z}_{2}\right| }^{p},\;{z}_{1},{z}_{2} \in  \mathbb{C},$$
$$

where $ C $ is a constant independent of $ {z}_{1} $ and $ {z}_{2} $ .

Lemma 3.2. Let $ \frac{\alpha }{4} < \sigma \leq \frac{1}{2} $ and $ F $ be $ {2\sigma } $ -Hölder continuous. For any $ v \in {H}_{\text{per }}^{1}\left( \Omega \right) $ , we have $ F\left( v\right) \in {H}_{\text{per }}^{\frac{\alpha }{2}}\left( \Omega \right) $ and

$$
$$\parallel F\left( v\right) {\parallel }_{{H}^{\frac{\alpha }{2}}} \lesssim  \parallel v{\parallel }_{{H}^{1}}^{2\sigma }. \tag{3.0.23}$$
$$

Proof. From Lemma 2.1 in (Zhang & Wang, 2024), recalling that $ \Omega $ is equipped with the periodic boundary condition, we have

$$
$$|F(v)|_{H^{\frac{\alpha}{2}}}^{2} \lesssim \int_{\Omega} \int_{\Omega} \frac{|F(v(x + y)) - F(v(y))|^2}{|x|^{1+\alpha}} dx dy$$
$$

$$
$$\lesssim \int_{\Omega} \int_{\Omega} \frac{|v(x + y) - v(y)|^{4\sigma}}{|x|^{1+\alpha}} dx dy \quad (F \text{ is } 2\sigma-\text{Hölder continuous})$$
$$

$$
$$= \int_{\Omega} \int_{\Omega} \frac{|\int_0^1 v^{\prime}(y + \theta x) x d\theta|^{4\sigma}}{|x|^{1+\alpha}} dx dy$$
$$

$$
$$\leq \int_{\Omega} \int_{\Omega} \int_0^1 |v^{\prime}(y + \theta x)|^{4\sigma} d\theta \frac{|x|^{4\sigma}}{|x|^{1+\alpha}} dx dy\tag{3.0.24}$$
$$

$$
$$\lesssim\int_{\Omega} \frac{|x|^{4\sigma}}{|x|^{1+\alpha}} dx \int_{\Omega} \int_0^1 |v^{\prime}(y)|^{4\sigma} d\theta dy~\text{(change integration order)}$$
$$

$$
$$= \int_{\Omega} \frac{|x|^{4\sigma}}{|x|^{1+\alpha}} dx \, \|v^{\prime}\|_{L^{4\sigma}}^{4\sigma}$$
$$

$$
$$\lesssim \int_{\Omega} \frac{|x|^{4\sigma}}{|x|^{1+\alpha}} dx \, \|v\|_{H^1}^{4\sigma} \quad (\sigma \leq \frac{1}{2})$$
$$

$$
$$\lesssim \|v\|_{H^1}^{4\sigma} \quad (\sigma > \frac{\alpha}{4}, \text{ the integral } \int_{\Omega} \frac{|x|^{4\sigma}}{|x|^{1+\alpha}} dx ~\text{ converges}).$$
$$

Thus, we have completed the proof.
