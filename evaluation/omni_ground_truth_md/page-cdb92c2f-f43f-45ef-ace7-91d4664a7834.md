Let us consider that $ \left( {\mathcal{L},\left\lbrack {{ \cdot }_{\lambda } \cdot }\right\rbrack ,\alpha }\right) $ is a Hom-Leibniz conformal algebra, which has coefficients in the representation $ \left( {l,r,\mathcal{M},\beta }\right) $ . The group of $ {nth} $ -cochain $ {C}_{HomL}^{n}\left( {\mathcal{L},M}\right) $ contains the map $ {f}_{{\lambda }_{1},\cdots ,{\lambda }_{n - 1}} : { \land }^{\otimes n}\mathcal{L} \rightarrow \mathbb{C}\left\lbrack {{\lambda }_{1},\cdots ,{\lambda }_{n - 1}}\right\rbrack \otimes \mathcal{M} $ defined by $ {p}_{1} \otimes {p}_{2} \otimes \cdots \otimes {p}_{n} \mapsto {f}_{{\lambda }_{1},{\lambda }_{2},\cdots ,{\lambda }_{n - 1}}\left( {{p}_{1},{p}_{2},\cdots ,{p}_{n}}\right) $ . These maps satisfy the following conformal sequilinearity condition:

$$
$$f_{\lambda_1, \lambda_2, \ldots, \lambda_{n-1}}(p_1, p_2, \ldots, \partial(p_i), \ldots, p_n)$$
$$

$$
$$= \left\{  \begin{array}{ll}  - {\lambda }_{i}{f}_{{\lambda }_{1},{\lambda }_{2},\cdots ,{\lambda }_{n - 1}}\left( {{p}_{1},{p}_{2},\cdots ,{p}_{n}}\right) , & i = 1,\cdots n-1,\\  \left( {\partial  + {\lambda }_{1} + {\lambda }_{2} + \cdots  + {\lambda }_{n - 1}}\right) {f}_{{\lambda }_{1},{\lambda }_{2},\cdots ,{\lambda }_{n - 1}}\left( {{p}_{1},{p}_{2},\cdots ,{p}_{n}}\right) , & i = n. \end{array}\right.  \tag{12}$$
$$

such that $ f \circ {\alpha }^{\otimes n} = \beta \circ f $. The coboundary map $ {\delta }_{Hom} : {C}_{Hom}^{n}\left( {\mathcal{L},\mathcal{M}}\right) \rightarrow {C}_{Hom}^{n + 1}\left( {\mathcal{L},\mathcal{M}}\right) $ can be defined by

$$
$$(\delta _{HomL} f)_{\lambda_1, \cdots, \lambda_n}(p_1, \cdots, p_{n+1})$$
$$

$$
$$= \sum_{i=1}^{n} (-1)^{i+1} l(\alpha^{n-1}(p_i))_{\lambda_i} f_{\lambda_1, \cdots, \hat{\lambda}_i, \cdots, \lambda_n}(p_1, \cdots, \hat{p}_i, \cdots, p_{n+1})\tag{13}$$
$$

$$
$$+ (-1)^{n+1} r(f_{\lambda_1, \cdots, \lambda_{n-1}}(p_1, \cdots, p_n))_{\lambda_1 + \cdots + \lambda_n} \alpha^{n-1}(p_{n+1})$$
$$

$$
$$+ \sum_{i < j} (-1)^{i+j} f_{\lambda_i + \lambda_j, \lambda_1, \cdots, \hat{\lambda}_i, \cdots, \hat{\lambda}_j, \cdots, \lambda_n}([p_{i_{\lambda_i}}  p_j], \alpha(p_1), \cdots, \hat{\alpha(p_i)}, \cdots, \hat{\alpha(p_j)}, \cdots, \alpha(p_{n+1})),$$
$$

where $ f \in {C}_{HomL}^{n}\left( {\mathcal{L},\mathcal{M}}\right) $ and $ {p}_{1},{p}_{2},\cdots ,{p}_{n},{p}_{n + 1} \in \mathcal{L} $ . With coefficients in the representation $ \left( {l,r,\mathcal{M},\beta }\right) $, the corresponding cohomology groups are known as the cohomology of the Hom-Leibniz conformal algebra $ \left( {\mathcal{L},\left\lbrack { \cdot }_{\lambda } { \cdot }\right\rbrack ,\alpha }\right) $ . If we assume that $ l{\left( p\right) }_{\lambda }m = \left\lbrack {{p}_{\lambda }m}\right\rbrack $ and $ r{\left( m\right) }_{\lambda }p = \left\lbrack {{m}_{\lambda }p}\right\rbrack $ then above equations turns to be

$$
$$(\delta _{HomL} f)_{\lambda_1, \cdots, \lambda_n}(p_1, \cdots, p_{n+1})$$
$$

$$
$$= \sum_{i=1}^{n} (-1)^{i+1} [\alpha^{n-1}(p_i)_{\lambda_i} f_{\lambda_1, \cdots, \hat{\lambda}_i, \cdots, \lambda_n}(p_1, \cdots, \hat{p}_i, \cdots, p_{n+1})\tag{14}]$$
$$

$$
$$+ (-1)^{n+1} (f_{\lambda_1, \cdots, \lambda_{n-1}}(p_1, \cdots, p_n)_{\lambda_1 + \cdots + \lambda_n} \alpha^{n-1}(p_{n+1})]$$
$$

$$
$$+ \sum_{i < j} (-1)^{i+j} f_{\lambda_i + \lambda_j, \lambda_1, \cdots, \hat{\lambda}_i, \cdots, \hat{\lambda}_j, \cdots, \lambda_n}([p_{i_{\lambda_i }}, p_j], \alpha(p_1), \cdots, \hat{\alpha(p_i)}, \cdots, \hat{\alpha(p_j)}, \cdots, \alpha(p_{n+1})),$$
$$

Theorem 3.1. $ {\delta }_{HomL}^{2} \circ {\delta }_{HomL}^{1} = 0 $ .

*Proof.* Consider that for $ n = 1 $ , we have

$$
$$(\delta^1_{HomL} f)(p_1, p_2) = l(p_1)_\lambda f(p_2) + r(f(p_1))_\lambda p_2 - f([p_{1_ \lambda}p_2])$$
$$

$$
$$= [p_{1_ \lambda} f(p_2)] + [f(p_1) {}_\lambda p_2] - f([p_{1_ \lambda} p_2]).$$
$$

and

$$
$$(\delta^2_{HomL} f)_{\lambda_1, \lambda_2}(p_1, p_2, p_3) = l(\alpha(p_1))_{\lambda_1} f_{\lambda_2}(p_2, p_3) - l(\alpha(p_2))_{\lambda_2} f_{\lambda_1}(p_1, p_3)$$
$$

$$
$$- r(f_{\lambda_1}(p_1, p_2))_{\lambda_1 + \lambda_2} \alpha(p_3) - f_{\lambda_1 + \lambda_2}([p_{1_ {\lambda_1}} p_2], \alpha(p_3))$$
$$

$$
$$+ f_{\lambda_1 + \lambda_2}([p_{1_ {\lambda_1}} p_3], \alpha(p_2)) - f_{\lambda_1 + \lambda_2}([p_{2_ {\lambda_2}}p_3], \alpha(p_1))\tag{15}$$

$$= [(\alpha(p_1))_{\lambda_1} f_{\lambda_2}(p_2, p_3)] - [(\alpha(p_2))_{\lambda_2} f_{\lambda_1}(p_1, p_3)]$$

$$
$$-[(f_{\lambda_1}(p_1, p_2))_{\lambda_1 + \lambda_2} \alpha(p_3)] - f_{\lambda_1 + \lambda_2}([p_{1_ {\lambda_1}} p_2], \alpha(p_3))$$
